I am a professor at the Department of Computer Science and Media Technology of Linnaeus University in Sweden, and a Chief Scientist at the Darwin research group of NORCE in Norway. My main research interests are in software engineering of self-adaptive systems. Self-adaptation endows a software system with the capability to adapt itself to deal with uncertain operating conditions, such as dynamic availability of resources, faults that are difficult to predict, and changing user goals. We are particularly interested in how we can exploit design models, verification techniques, and AI techniques at runtime to provide assurances for the required adaptation goals. I champion solid validation of research results through establised scientific methods, such as design science, controlled experiments, and case studies. I received a PhD from form the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven, Belgium in 2006 for work on software archtitecture for multi-agent systems. After my PhD, I was postdoc fellow, funded by the Research Foundation Flanders. From 2015 until 2025, I was a professor at the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven where was a member of the imec-DistriNet reearch group.


   In 2020, my book Introduction to Self-Adaptive Systems: A Contemporary Software Engineering Perspective was published by Wiley. This book offers a concise and practical introduction to the foundations and engineering principles of self-adaptation. It provides readers with an accessible set of basic principles, engineering foundations, and applications of self-adaptation in software-intensive systems.
